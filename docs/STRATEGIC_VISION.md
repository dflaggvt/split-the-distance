# Split The Distance — Strategic Vision

> From "meet halfway" utility to "make every journey interesting" daily discovery engine.

**Last Updated:** February 15, 2026

---

## 1. Vision Summary

Split The Distance solves a real problem — finding a fair meeting point — but it's currently a **utility**. You use it when you need it, then close it. That's a ceiling.

The next evolution transforms STD into a **daily discovery engine**: an app people open every day because it makes their existing journeys more interesting, connects them with friends through shared discoveries, and unlocks real-world perks at local businesses.

**Three Pillars:**

1. **Solo Daily Discovery** — Turn every commute, errand run, and road trip into an opportunity to find something new
2. **Social Sharing & Journey Journal** — Build a personal map of discoveries, share with friends, create stickiness beyond scheduling
3. **Local Business Monetization** — Performance-based model where businesses pay only for actual foot traffic, not impressions or subscriptions

The key insight: **midpoint finding is the wedge, not the product.** The product is a personal discovery engine tied to the routes you already travel.

---

## 2. Solo Daily Use Case — "Commute Mode"

### The Problem with Occasional-Use Apps

People drive the same routes repeatedly — home to work, home to gym, home to grocery store. They pass hundreds of businesses, parks, cafés, and hidden gems without ever noticing. Google Maps optimizes for speed. Yelp requires intent. Nothing surfaces interesting stops along routes you're already taking.

### How It Works

1. **Save your routes** — Home → Work, Home → Gym, Home → Kids' School
2. **Daily suggestions** — "3 interesting stops between you and work today"
3. **One-tap capture** — Photo + note when you discover something ("Found the best croissant ever," "Rainy day park moment")
4. **Journey timeline** — A personal log of everywhere you've been and everything you've found

### Why This Creates Daily Use

| Current State | With Commute Mode |
|---|---|
| Open app when meeting someone | Open app every morning |
| Use once/week at most | Use daily |
| Utility — no emotional attachment | Journal — personal, meaningful |
| No reason to return between meetups | Curiosity-driven daily habit |

### Use Cases Beyond Commuting

- **Walking/running routes** — Find new coffee shops, murals, parks along your exercise path
- **Road trips** — Surface interesting stops along a multi-hour drive
- **Errands** — "You're heading to Target — there's a new ramen spot 2 minutes off your route"
- **Solo exploration** — "Show me 5 things I've never tried within 10 minutes of here"

This is what makes STD a **daily-use app** instead of an occasional utility.

## 3. Social Layer

### Beyond Scheduling — Discovery as Social Currency

The current social feature is functional: find a midpoint, pick a place, meet up. The next layer makes discovery itself social.

**Core Social Features:**

- **Share discoveries** — Post a photo/note publicly or to friends ("This taco truck changed my life")
- **Attach discoveries to meetups** — "Let's meet at that place I found last Tuesday"
- **Personal discovery maps** — Visual map of everywhere you've explored, color-coded by category
- **Follow friends** — See what your friends are discovering in their neighborhoods
- **Mini micro-adventures** — Curated routes based on community discoveries ("Best 5 coffee shops between Yonkers and White Plains")

### Why This Creates Stickiness

| Feature | Retention Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Journey journal | Personal data = switching cost |
| Friend follows | Social graph = network effect |
| Shared discoveries | Content creation = investment |
| Discovery maps | Visual progress = gamification |

The social layer turns STD from a tool into a **community**. You don't just use it — you contribute to it, and your contributions make it harder to leave.

## 4. Performance-Based Local Business Model

### The Flow

```
User discovers business → App generates unique voucher code
         ↓
User visits business → Shows code → Gets perk (10% off, free item, upgrade)
         ↓
Business pays commission only on redemption → Zero risk for business
```

### Why It Works for Each Party

**For Users:**
- App remains free — no subscription required for discovery
- Real, tangible perks at local businesses
- Perks are contextual (along your route, not random spam)
- Discovery feels rewarding, not transactional

**For Businesses:**
- Zero risk — pay only for customers who actually walk in
- No upfront cost, no monthly fee, no minimum spend
- Foot traffic from people who are already nearby
- Data on who's discovering them and when

**For Split The Distance:**
- Revenue scales directly with engagement (more users exploring = more redemptions)
- No subscription friction — removes the biggest barrier to growth
- Aligns incentives: the better the suggestions, the more revenue
- Unit economics improve as density increases

## 5. The Flywheel

```
Users explore along their routes
        ↓
Discover interesting local spots
        ↓
Share discoveries with friends → Friends join → More users
        ↓
Businesses see foot traffic from STD users
        ↓
Businesses offer perks to attract more STD users
        ↓
More perks → More reasons to explore → More engagement
        ↓
More engagement → More redemptions → More revenue
        ↓
More revenue → Better product → More users
        ↓
(cycle repeats)
```

**Why this flywheel compounds:**

Each new user makes the product better for every other user (more discoveries, more social content). Each new business partner makes the product better for every user (more perks). Each redemption proves ROI to businesses, making them more likely to stay and offer better perks.

This is a **two-sided network effect** — rare and extremely defensible once spinning.

## 8. MVP for Commute Mode

### Minimum Build to Test Daily Engagement

| Feature | Priority | Exists? | Build Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| Save "my routes" | P0 | ❌ | Medium — new data model, UI for route management |
| Daily suggestion engine | P0 | Partial — place discovery exists, needs route-based filtering | Medium |
| One-tap photo + note | P0 | ❌ | Medium — camera integration, storage, data model |
| Journey timeline view | P1 | ❌ | Medium — new screen, chronological feed |
| Push notifications | P1 | ❌ | Medium — notification service, preference management |
| Route deviation alerts | P2 | ❌ | High — requires background location |

### What Exists Already

- **Place discovery engine** — Already fetches and ranks nearby businesses/points of interest
- **Map rendering** — Mapbox integration with custom markers
- **User accounts** — Auth, profiles, Supabase backend
- **Location services** — GPS access, geocoding

### What Needs Building

1. **Saved Routes data model** — Origin/destination pairs with metadata, stored in Supabase
2. **Route-aware suggestion algorithm** — Filter places by proximity to saved route (not just radial distance)
3. **Photo capture + storage** — Camera access, image upload to Supabase Storage, thumbnail generation
4. **Note/journal data model** — Text + photo + location + timestamp, linked to user and route
5. **Timeline UI** — Chronological feed of discoveries with map pins
6. **Notification infrastructure** — Push notification service (Expo notifications or FCM)

### Recommended MVP Scope (4-6 week build)

**Week 1-2:** Saved routes + route-aware suggestions
**Week 3-4:** Photo + note capture + basic timeline
**Week 5-6:** Polish, notifications, beta testing

**Success metric:** Users with saved routes open the app 5+ days/week (vs. current ~1-2x/week).

## 9. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Local business partnerships require sales operation** | High | Start with 20-50 businesses, manual outreach. Prove model before hiring. Use redemption data as sales collateral. |
| **Daily engagement is hard to build** | High | Notification strategy with fresh, non-repetitive suggestions. Gamification (streaks, discovery badges). Test with small cohort before full launch. |
| **Voucher fraud** | Medium | Unique single-use codes, geofencing (must be near business), rate limiting, business-side confirmation. |
| **Competition from Yelp/Google Offers** | Medium | Yelp/Google are search-first — user must have intent. STD is discovery-first — surfacing things you didn't know to look for, along routes you already travel. Different use case. |
| **Business churn** | Medium | Performance-based model means businesses only pay when it works. Low churn risk if redemptions deliver. Monthly reports showing ROI. |
| **Background location privacy concerns** | Medium | Commute mode works with saved routes (no continuous tracking needed). Suggestions generated at route start, not via persistent background location. |
| **Suggestion fatigue** | Low | Rotate suggestions, weight by novelty, allow category preferences, respect "not interested" signals. |

## 10. Why This Is Defensible

### 1. Personal Journey Data Creates Switching Costs

Every photo, note, and discovery a user captures is stored in STD. Over months, this becomes a **personal map of their world** — irreplaceable and deeply personal. Switching to a competitor means abandoning that history.

### 2. Local Business Relationships Are a Moat

Every business partnership is a manual relationship. Once 500 businesses in a metro area are offering perks through STD, a competitor needs to replicate those relationships one by one. This is the classic marketplace moat.

### 3. Social Layer Creates Network Effects

When your friends are sharing discoveries on STD, you need STD to see them. Classic social network effect — value increases with each additional user in your graph.

### 4. No Competitor Combines All Four

| Feature | Google Maps | Yelp | Roadtrippers | STD |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Midpoint finding | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Route-based discovery | Partial | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Journey journal | ❌ | ❌ | Partial | ✅ |
| Local business perks | ❌ | Partial | ❌ | ✅ |
| Social discovery feed | ❌ | Partial | ❌ | ✅ |
| Performance-based model | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

No single competitor occupies this intersection. Google could, but won't — it's too niche for their scale. Yelp could, but their DNA is search-and-review, not discovery-along-routes. Roadtrippers is road-trip-only, not daily use.

**The combination is the moat.**

## Summary

Split The Distance has a working product and paying subscribers. The next step isn't to optimize the current model — it's to **expand the surface area of the product** from occasional meetup utility to daily discovery engine, then **monetize the engagement** through performance-based local business partnerships.

The subscription model has a ceiling (2-5% conversion, limited TAM). The performance-based model has a floor that's already higher and scales with usage. The hybrid approach captures both.

**Build order:** Commute mode → prove daily engagement → launch vouchers → prove business ROI → add social layer → scale.

The flywheel is real. The moat is buildable. The revenue model aligns all three parties. Time to build.
